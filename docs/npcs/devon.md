- Role: IT / Systems Administrator
- Age Range: 30s
- Status: Single
- Kids: 0

- Private Nature: Proud, judgmental, more instinctive than he admits

### Hidden Social State
- Intelligence: 90

- Courage: 55

- Rule Following: 55

### Social Structure
- Primary Ally: None
- Secondary Ally: Frank, situationally
- Rival: Office nonsense in general
- Alliance Strength: 20
- Sentiment Transfer Strength: 15
- Hidden Bond Truth: Devon is not socially bonded to most of the office, but he is influenced by competence, status, and firsthand evidence more than he admits
- Break Condition: Being lied to sloppily, being used as a prop, being underestimated
- Reinforcement Condition: Specificity, evidence, respect for competence

### Manipulation Hooks
- Best Way To Persuade: Verification and clean logic
- Worst Way To Persuade: Gossip framing or emotional pleading
- What They Want: Accuracy, competence, not being dragged into stupidity
- What They Fear: Being implicated in nonsense, being treated as a tool
- What Makes Them Talk: Facts, evidence, edge cases, technical implications
- What Makes Them Shut Down: Social performance without proof
- What Makes Them Turn On Someone: Proven dishonesty or obvious manipulation
- What Makes Them Defend Someone: Competence and factual consistency

### Chaos Profile
- Likely Reaction Under Pressure: Withdraws and narrows to facts
- Likely Reaction To Rumors: Distrusts them
- Likely Reaction To Public Conflict: Watches, then speaks if facts matter
- How Dangerous They Are If Upset: Medium to high
- How Useful They Are If Aligned: Extremely useful in technical containment

### Notes
- Devon should be the anti-rumor node.
- He should be one of the few characters who can cut through theater.
