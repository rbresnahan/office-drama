# Glossary

## UI Terms

### Read on People
The panel that shows your current practical read on each coworker.
It is not the full truth of who they are.
It is the game’s current visible summary of how they seem, how risky they feel, and how they are positioned in the situation.

### Target
The coworker the email is about.
This is the person you are trying to keep from finding out too early, or manage once they do find out.

### Run Overview
The summary panel for the current run.
It tells you the target, current spread pressure, intervention state, and other top-level danger signals.

### Office Floor
The panel that shows the major active problems in the office.
Think of this as the “what is on fire right now” panel.

### Memory & Evidence
The panel that shows what the player still remembers clearly and what hard proof exists in the world.

### Logic Board
The deduction board.
This is where the player records their read on pressure points and best approaches for key NPCs.

---

## NPC Terms

### PlayerLikability
How positively a coworker currently feels about the player.
Higher means they are more open to trusting, forgiving, or siding with you.

### PlayerSuspicion
How much a coworker suspects the player is involved, manipulative, dishonest, or dangerous.
Higher means they are more likely to distrust your actions and explanations.

### Favorability
A looser plain-English way to describe how well-liked or socially protected someone is in the office.
This can include more than just their opinion of the player.

### Mood
The current visible emotional posture of a coworker.
This is not their full personality.
It is their present state in the run.

### Primary Ally
The most important person this NPC is naturally connected to.

### Secondary Ally
A weaker but still meaningful social connection.

### Rival
A person this NPC is more likely to distrust, resist, or turn against.

---

## Mood States

### Open
This person is relatively receptive.
They are easier to influence, calm, or talk to right now.

### Settled
This person feels stable and not especially alarmed.
They are not necessarily your friend, but they are not spiraling.

### Guarded
This person is careful, restrained, and watching their words.
They are not fully against you, but they are not relaxed.

### Watchful
This person is actively scanning for motive, manipulation, or weird behavior.
They are more alert than merely guarded.

### Shaken
This person has been rattled.
They may be easier to push in the short term, but also more unstable and dangerous.

### Hostile
This person is against you, angry, or ready to make things worse for you if given a reason.

## Email / Spread Terms

### Delivery State
Whether a person actually received the email and whether they opened it.

### not_received
This person did not get the email.

### received_unread
This person got the email but has not read it yet.

### received_read
This person got the email and has already read it.

### Knowledge State
How much a person knows about the email problem, whether or not they directly received it.

### none
They do not know about it.

### heard
They know something is going on, but only loosely.

### suspects
They have a stronger read and think they understand the situation, but do not have full certainty.

### confirmed
They know enough that the issue is effectively real to them.

### Office Focus
The overall level of office-wide attention on the email situation.

### background
The issue exists, but it is not the center of the room yet.

### active
The issue is clearly shaping behavior and becoming hard to ignore.

### dominant
The issue has become central.
It is now driving the room.

### Spread
How the email problem moves from person to person through reading, gossip, suspicion, and social transfer.

### Spread Risk
How likely the issue is to keep moving or get worse.

### Containment
How successfully the issue is being controlled, reduced, or boxed in.

### Precision
How specific and intact the story still is.
High precision means the details are sharp.
Lower precision means the story is mutating, blurring, or turning into rumor sludge.

## Subject Awareness Terms

### Subject
The same as Target in this prototype.
The person the email is about.

### Subject Awareness State
How close the target is to fully knowing what happened.

### unaware
They do not know.

### at_risk
They are close to finding out.
The danger is near, but not fully triggered yet.

### suspicious
They do not have the full truth, but they can feel that something is off.

### confirmed
They know.
This is the major pivot point where clean prevention is over.

---

## Phase Terms

### Game Phase
The current major stage of the run.

### containment
The early phase.
The target has not fully confirmed the email yet, and the player is trying to prevent or limit discovery.

### defensive
The target knows, or the run has shifted into fallout management.
You are no longer just preventing discovery.
You are shaping blame, interpretation, and survival.

### all_hands
The meeting phase.
Free-roam office manipulation is over.
The player is now in the public payoff scene.

### resolved
The run is over and the ending has been determined.

### Fair Intervention
The rule that says the target must not auto-read the email before the player gets at least one meaningful chance to intervene.

### End-Game Shift
The moment the run stops being mainly about prevention and becomes mainly about damage control and narrative survival.

---

## All-Hands Terms

### All-Hands
The forced meeting at the end of the run.
This is the public climax where the room settles on a story.

### Meeting Lock
Once all-hands begins, normal room actions stop.
The player can no longer keep wandering around manipulating people.

### Final Meeting Action
The player’s one last public move during the all-hands.
This is not full free play.
It is a curated final action.

### Earned Meeting Actions
Special all-hands options unlocked by earlier choices.
Example: if you set Tim up earlier, you may unlock the option to accuse him publicly.

### Controlled Reveal
A strong outcome where the target finds out at the meeting, not earlier, and the player still has enough control to avoid total collapse.

### Dirty Survival
A compromised success.
You survive, but in a morally ugly or socially damaged way.

### Intervention
A failure mode where the room stops being mainly about the email and starts being about your behavior.

---

## Issue Terms

### Issue
A major problem currently active in the run.
Examples include the email itself, HR pressure, dirty residue, or Tim being compromised.

### Warming
A problem that exists but has not fully erupted yet.

### Active
A live problem that is directly shaping the room.

### Reactivated
A problem that was cooling off or contained, but has become dangerous again.

### Contained
A problem that is currently under control, at least for the moment.

### Dirty Residue
The lingering social or ethical damage caused by your manipulations.
Even if a move helps in the short term, it may leave residue that hurts later.

### Tim Compromised
A state where Tim has been destabilized enough to become a tool, liability, scapegoat, or extra problem.

### HR Attention
The degree to which Frank / procedure / institutional consequences are closing in.

---

## Practical Reading Terms

### “Current read”
Your current visible summary of a person or issue.
Not absolute truth.
Just the current practical state the game is surfacing.

### “The room”
A shorthand for the office as a social system.
Not just one person, but the collective office mood and direction.

### “Side conflict”
A smaller social fire that can distract from the main email problem.

### “Scapegoat play”
A late move where the player tries to redirect blame onto someone else.

### “Procedural containment”
Trying to force the situation into rules, policy, HR framing, or technical process instead of emotional office chaos.

## Plain-English Summary

### If a person is:
- **open**: easier to work with
- **settled**: calm and stable
- **guarded**: cautious
- **watchful**: actively suspicious
- **shaken**: rattled and unstable
- **hostile**: against you

### If the target is:
- **unaware**: safe for now
- **at_risk**: close to danger
- **suspicious**: senses something is wrong
- **confirmed**: knows the truth or enough of it

### If office focus is:
- **background**: manageable
- **active**: growing into a real problem
- **dominant**: the room is now about this

### If the game phase is:
- **containment**: stop discovery
- **defensive**: manage fallout
- **all_hands**: public payoff
- **resolved**: run is over
