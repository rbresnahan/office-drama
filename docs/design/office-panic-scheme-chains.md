# Office Panic Demo — Scheme Chains v0.1

## Purpose

This document defines the staged con-job logic for the Office Panic demo.

A scheme chain is a multi-step plan the player builds across phases.

Each scheme should feel earned.

The player should not be able to jump directly to the payoff without discovering vulnerabilities, seeding belief, creating evidence, or taking risks.

---

# Scheme Chain Template

Use this structure for every major scheme.

## Scheme Name

### Target

Who or what the scheme affects.

### Goal

What the player wants to accomplish.

### Why It Matters

How this scheme helps the player survive the all-hands.

### Required Discovery

What the player must learn before the route becomes available.

### Phase Availability

When the scheme can be discovered, advanced, committed to, and resolved.

### Progress Stages

#### 0%

Initial state.

#### 25%

First setup beat.

#### 50%

Second setup beat.

#### 75%

Commitment or evidence beat.

#### 100%

Payoff state.

### Example Actions

Actions that advance the scheme.

### Risks

Red bars or consequences that may increase.

### Partial Success Outcomes

What happens if the scheme ends below 100%.

### Full Success Outcome

What happens if the scheme reaches 100%.

### Failure Outcome

What happens if the scheme collapses or backfires.

### Cross-Scheme Tie-Ins

Ways this scheme connects to other schemes.

# Scheme 1: Frame Frank

## Target

Frank.

## Goal

Make Frank believable as the source of the disaster, or at least as someone unstable enough to absorb blame.

## Why It Matters

Frank can become a scapegoat.

If Frank becomes suspicious enough, the all-hands may focus on him instead of the player.

The player does not need everyone to fully believe Frank caused the disaster.

The player only needs the room to believe he could have.

## Required Discovery

The player must learn or create a reason why Frank could be viewed as unstable, careless, angry, or compromised.

Possible discoveries:

- Frank has been under pressure.
- Frank has a prior drinking issue.
- Frank was away from his desk.
- Frank had a reason to dislike Celia.
- Frank had access to something suspicious.
- Someone already thinks Frank has been acting strange.

The drinking version of this route should require discovered context before the commitment move feels available.

The player should have:

- Seen or taken the bottle.
- Confirmed Frank absence, availability, or desk access context.
- Found at least one social discovery or suspicion source.

Valid social sources include:

- Betty noting Frank was away.
- Betty hearing Frank suspicion.
- Lisa having HR or process context.
- Devon repeating gossip.

The story data does not need a new system for this. Use existing facts, flags, requirements, unlocks, feedback, latest signal, bars, and used choices to make the route feel earned before the bottle becomes a commitment move.

## Phase Availability

### Phase 1

Discover Frank vulnerability.

### Phase 2

Seed suspicion with Betty, Tim, Devon, or Lisa.

### Phase 3

Create supporting evidence or push a witness.

### Phase 4

Commit to the Frank story.

### Finale

Frank may absorb blame, fight back, or expose the player.

---

## Progress Stages

### 0%

Nobody suspects Frank.

Frank is just another person in the office.

Planting evidence now would feel random and dangerous.

### 25%

Someone hears that Frank has been acting strange.

The idea is now in the room.

Possible carrier:

- Betty.
- Devon.
- Tim.
- Lisa.
- The player.

### 50%

A second person is watching Frank.

Frank’s normal behavior starts being interpreted through suspicion.

Ordinary things now look weird.

### 75%

There is evidence near Frank.

This could be:

- A bottle.
- A suspicious absence.
- A forwarded email.
- Someone seeing him near the wrong desk.
- Someone remembering he was upset earlier.
- A rumor that he had been drinking.

### 100%

At the all-hands, Frank is a believable scapegoat.

People may not fully believe he did it, but they believe he could have.

That is enough.

---

## Example Actions

- Ask Betty if Frank seemed off today.
- Tell Tim that Frank was away from his desk around the time the email spread.
- Mention to Devon that Frank has been under pressure.
- Plant the bottle in Frank’s drawer.
- Suggest Frank may have forwarded the email without understanding what it was.
- Leave the bottle somewhere visible but deniable.
- Ask someone whether Frank has been drinking again.
- Suggest Frank had a reason to be angry with Celia.

---

## Risks

- Frank Retaliates increases.
- Tim Suspects You increases if the timeline is sloppy.
- Betty Loses Trust increases if she thinks the player is steering her.
- Management Escalates increases if the accusation gets serious.
- Celia may sympathize with Frank if the attack feels cruel.

---

## Partial Success Outcomes

### 25%

Frank becomes part of the office chatter, but not a serious suspect.

### 50%

Frank creates uncertainty, but cannot fully absorb blame.

### 75%

Frank becomes a serious suspect, but may fight back.

### 100%

Frank is a viable scapegoat at the all-hands.

The player may survive, but the social cost can be high.

---

## Full Success Outcome

Frank becomes the room’s most plausible alternate explanation.

The player survives the central accusation.

If Frank Retaliates is high, Frank may still attack the player.

If Betty Loses Trust is high, Betty may realize the player engineered the outcome.

---

## Failure Outcome

Frank realizes he is being framed.

Frank attacks the player publicly.

Possible failure text:

Frank does not go down quietly.

He laughs once, sharp and ugly, then asks why the player was the first person to mention his desk.

The room turns.

The player survives the first accusation.

They do not survive the silence after it.

---

## Cross-Scheme Tie-Ins

- Blame the System can muddy how the email spread.
- Distract Tim can prevent Tim from disproving the Frank timeline.
- Sideline Tim can remove the strongest factual threat.
- Betty Klepto can explain missing or moved evidence.
- Devon Leak can help spread Frank suspicion.
- Warm Betty may conflict with Frame Frank if Betty sees the player being cruel.

# Scheme 2: Warm Betty

## Target

Betty.

## Goal

Make Betty sympathetic enough to protect the player, defend the player’s intent, or soften the room against the player.

## Why It Matters

Betty can serve as an emotional witness.

If Betty believes the player is overwhelmed rather than malicious, she may help soften the all-hands.

Betty is useful because she can make the player seem human.

## Required Discovery

No special discovery required.

Warm Betty should be available early.

However, stronger Betty choices may require:

- The player admitting partial truth.
- Betty seeing the player panic.
- The player asking for advice instead of help.
- The player not having betrayed Betty too aggressively.

## Phase Availability

### Phase 1

Build initial sympathy.

### Phase 2

Ask for advice or confess partial truth.

### Phase 3

Betty may ask harder questions.

### Phase 4

Ask Betty to defend the player.

### Finale

Betty may protect, hesitate, or turn against the player.

---

## Progress Stages

### 0%

Betty is neutral.

She may like the player, but she is not invested.

### 25%

Betty sees the player as overwhelmed instead of malicious.

She thinks the player made a mistake, not a deliberate attack.

### 50%

Betty believes there is context people are missing.

She may not defend the email, but she defends the idea that the player is not a monster.

### 75%

Betty is willing to speak up for the player.

She may interrupt Tim, soften Celia, or redirect the conversation.

### 100%

Betty actively protects the player at the all-hands.

She becomes the player’s human shield.

This is useful and morally gross.

Perfect office behavior, unfortunately.

---

## Example Actions

- Tell Betty you feel sick about what happened.
- Ask Betty for advice instead of asking her to help.
- Admit part of the truth before she hears it from someone else.
- Tell Betty you are worried Celia will think you hate her.
- Give Betty a small truth so she believes the larger lie.
- Let Betty see the player panic in a controlled way.
- Ask Betty what the right thing to do is.
- Say the player wants to apologize but is afraid of making it worse.

---

## Risks

- Betty Loses Trust increases if she catches manipulation.
- Celia Finds Out can increase if Betty checks on Celia.
- Tim Suspects You can increase if Betty talks to Tim.
- Warm Betty can drop if the player is caught framing Frank too aggressively.
- Betty may become emotionally invested, then feel betrayed later.

---

## Partial Success Outcomes

### 25%

Betty is softer toward the player, but not protective.

### 50%

Betty may slow down criticism, but will not fully defend the player.

### 75%

Betty may defend the player once.

### 100%

Betty actively protects the player during the all-hands.

---

## Full Success Outcome

Betty defends the player’s intent during the all-hands.

She may say the player made a mistake, but is not malicious.

This can reduce the emotional force of Celia’s accusation.

---

## Failure Outcome

Betty realizes she was used.

If Betty Loses Trust reaches 100%, Betty refuses to defend the player and may expose the player’s manipulation.

---

## Cross-Scheme Tie-Ins

- Warm Betty can soften Contain Celia.
- Warm Betty can conflict with Frame Frank.
- Warm Betty can conflict with Betty Klepto.
- Betty may become a carrier for Frank suspicion.
- Betty may accidentally increase Celia Finds Out if she checks on Celia.

# Scheme 3: Distract Tim

## Target

Tim.

## Goal

Keep Tim from reconstructing the actual email timeline.

## Why It Matters

Tim is dangerous because he is procedural.

He is not simply angry.

He collects facts.

If Tim has a clean timeline at the all-hands, the player is exposed.

## Required Discovery

The player must learn what Tim is investigating or what kind of details Tim cares about.

Possible discoveries:

- Tim is checking recall logs.
- Tim is comparing when people saw the email.
- Tim is asking who forwarded what.
- Tim is looking at desk presence.
- Tim is trying to reconstruct the timeline.

## Phase Availability

### Phase 1

Learn Tim is dangerous.

### Phase 2

Send Tim toward a wrong detail.

### Phase 3

Pollute the timeline.

### Phase 4

Commit to a false theory or remove Tim’s certainty.

### Finale

Tim may expose the player, hesitate, or be neutralized.

---

## Progress Stages

### 0%

Tim is focused on the actual timeline.

This is bad.

### 25%

Tim is chasing one wrong detail.

Maybe he is checking:

- Recall logs.
- Forwarding rules.
- Who was at their desk.
- Whether the email thread split.
- Whether Devon forwarded the message.

### 50%

Tim has two competing theories.

He is less certain.

### 75%

Tim’s timeline is polluted.

He has facts, but the facts point in several directions.

### 100%

At the all-hands, Tim cannot confidently prove what happened.

He may suspect the player, but suspicion is not a clean accusation.

---

## Example Actions

- Ask Tim whether email recall logs are reliable.
- Suggest Devon forwards everything without reading.
- Mention Frank was away from his desk.
- Act confused about the exact send time.
- Ask Tim a technical question that sends him digging in the wrong place.
- Suggest the email may have been forwarded from an old thread.
- Ask whether multiple versions of the message exist.
- Give Tim one true detail that leads to the wrong conclusion.

---

## Risks

- Tim Suspects You increases if the player asks too many leading questions.
- Blame the System can increase, but Management Escalates may also increase.
- Frame Frank can increase if Tim starts checking Frank.
- Betty Loses Trust can increase if Betty hears the player changing the story.
- Tim may become more dangerous if he realizes he is being steered.

---

## Partial Success Outcomes

### 25%

Tim wastes a little time.

### 50%

Tim is uncertain, but still dangerous.

### 75%

Tim cannot move cleanly, but may suspect manipulation.

### 100%

Tim cannot prove the player caused the disaster during the all-hands.

---

## Full Success Outcome

Tim cannot deliver a clean factual takedown.

He may still dislike or suspect the player, but he cannot prove the player’s story is false in the meeting.

---

## Failure Outcome

Tim realizes the player tried to steer him.

Tim Suspects You rises sharply.

Tim may confront the player before or during the all-hands.

---

## Cross-Scheme Tie-Ins

- Frame Frank can redirect Tim’s investigation.
- Blame the System can overload Tim with technical uncertainty.
- Devon Leak can create alternate forwarding paths.
- Sideline Tim can remove Tim from the meeting entirely.
- Warm Betty can reduce the emotional impact of Tim’s facts.

---

# Scheme 4: Sideline Tim

## Target

Tim.

## Goal

Make Tim miss or weaken his role in the all-hands.

## Why It Matters

Tim is the timeline threat.

If Tim misses the meeting or arrives late, the player has more room to survive.

This is an underhanded physical/social sabotage route.

It should be petty, risky, and darkly funny.

## Required Discovery

The player must learn Tim has a food-related vulnerability or strict lunch routine.

Possible discoveries:

- Betty mentions Tim is careful about lunch.
- Tim complains about people touching his food.
- Devon jokes about Tim’s special lunch.
- The player notices Tim’s labeled lunch.
- Tim avoids certain food or drink.

## Phase Availability

### Phase 1

Discover Tim’s vulnerability.

### Phase 2

Create lunch confusion.

### Phase 3

Compromise Tim’s backup plan or comfort.

### Phase 4

Tim is delayed, weakened, or absent.

### Finale

Tim may miss the all-hands or arrive late.

---

## Progress Stages

### 0%

Tim is fully available and dangerous.

### 25%

The player learns Tim has a vulnerability or rigid food routine.

This unlocks future options.

### 50%

The player creates lunch confusion.

This could be a swap, mislabeling, distraction, or someone else grabbing the wrong item.

Keep this fictional and abstract.

The point is story logic, not real-world instruction.

### 75%

The player compromises Tim’s fallback comfort or exit plan.

This could involve bathroom logistics, office supplies, or timing.

This should feel risky and nasty.

### 100%

Tim misses the all-hands, arrives late, or is too distracted to present a clean timeline.

---

## Example Actions

- Ask Betty why Tim labels his lunch so aggressively.
- Let Devon joke about Tim’s food restrictions.
- Create confusion around the break-room fridge.
- Encourage Devon to grab the wrong lunch bag.
- Make Tim’s backup plan unavailable.
- Nudge Tim into eating at the worst possible time.
- Keep Tim away from his notes before the meeting.

---

## Risks

- Tim Suspects You increases if Tim notices the player near his lunch or notes.
- Management Escalates increases if the incident becomes more than office gossip.
- Betty Loses Trust increases if she sees the cruelty.
- Office Chaos may increase if added as a future bar.
- Lisa may begin documenting workplace misconduct.

---

## Partial Success Outcomes

### 25%

The player knows Tim’s vulnerability but has not acted.

### 50%

Tim may be distracted, but still attends the meeting.

### 75%

Tim is late, uncomfortable, or less effective.

### 100%

Tim misses the key part of the meeting or arrives too late to control the room.

---

## Full Success Outcome

Tim is not present when the most damaging part of the all-hands unfolds.

The player gains room to sell another story.

---

## Failure Outcome

Tim realizes the player sabotaged him.

Tim returns furious and more focused.

If Tim Suspects You is high, this can become catastrophic.

---

## Cross-Scheme Tie-Ins

- Missing bathroom supplies can be blamed on Betty Klepto.
- Tim absence strengthens Frame Frank.
- Tim absence strengthens Blame the System.
- Devon can be used as the accidental lunch-confusion carrier.
- Lisa can escalate if she suspects workplace sabotage.
- Betty may turn on the player if she sees the player hurting Tim.

---

# Scheme 5: Contain Celia

## Target

Celia.

## Goal

Prevent Celia from becoming the emotional center of the room.

## Why It Matters

Celia is the target of the bad email.

If Celia fully understands the email and goes public, the player is in serious trouble.

Containing Celia does not mean Celia forgives the player.

It means Celia does not end the player in the room.

## Required Discovery

The player must learn what Celia knows.

Possible discoveries:

- Celia has heard something happened.
- Celia knows the email was about her.
- Celia has not seen the full message yet.
- Devon may be about to tell her.
- Betty may check on her.
- Tim may ask her for clarification.

## Phase Availability

### Phase 1

Avoid or test Celia’s knowledge.

### Phase 2

Control the first version Celia hears.

### Phase 3

Prevent Celia from getting the full message.

### Phase 4

Commit to apology, minimization, or redirection.

### Finale

Celia may confront, withdraw, hesitate, or expose the player.

---

## Progress Stages

### 0%

Celia is exposed to the rumor but does not know the full damage yet.

### 25%

Celia knows something happened, but not the whole insult.

### 50%

Celia thinks the email may have been careless rather than cruel.

### 75%

Celia is angry, but not ready to destroy the player publicly.

### 100%

At the all-hands, Celia does not become the central witness against the player.

---

## Example Actions

- Apologize early but vaguely.
- Tell Celia the email was written during a stressful moment and was not meant as a real judgment.
- Ask Betty to check on Celia.
- Try to talk to Celia before Devon reaches her.
- Admit one true part while hiding the worst part.
- Tell Celia people are exaggerating what happened.
- Say the message was part of a larger conversation, not a direct attack.
- Ask Celia not to read fragments without context.

---

## Risks

- Celia Finds Out increases if the player says too much.
- Betty Loses Trust increases if Betty realizes the player minimized the insult.
- Tim Suspects You increases if Celia repeats the explanation and it contradicts the player’s timeline.
- Frank Retaliates can increase if Celia talks to Frank.
- Celia may become more dangerous if she senses emotional management.

---

## Partial Success Outcomes

### 25%

Celia knows something is wrong, but has no full picture.

### 50%

Celia is hurt but uncertain.

### 75%

Celia is angry, but not ready to go public.

### 100%

Celia does not become the main threat at the all-hands.

---

## Full Success Outcome

Celia does not drive the all-hands against the player.

She may still be hurt.

She may still know enough to matter later.

But she does not detonate the room.

---

## Failure Outcome

Celia gets the full message and understands the player tried to manage her.

Celia Finds Out reaches 100%.

Celia can become the emotional center of the finale.

---

## Cross-Scheme Tie-Ins

- Warm Betty can help soften Celia.
- Devon Leak can be blamed for exaggerating what Celia heard.
- Blame the System can suggest Celia saw fragments.
- Celia Dramatic can undermine Celia, but is morally ugly and risky.
- Tim can confirm or disprove the player’s explanation.

---

# Scheme 6: Blame the System

## Target

The email system, office process, recall behavior, forwarding confusion, and workplace communication norms.

## Goal

Make the incident feel like a technical failure, forwarding mistake, recall glitch, or confusing office communication issue.

## Why It Matters

This is the cleanest-sounding survival route.

It can reduce personal blame.

It can also attract management attention.

## Required Discovery

The player must learn that people saw different versions, that recall only partly worked, or that Tim is investigating system behavior.

Possible discoveries:

- The recall only partly worked.
- Someone saw a preview but not the full email.
- Devon forwarded something.
- Tim is checking logs.
- People disagree about who saw what.
- The thread may have split.

## Phase Availability

### Phase 1

Learn recall failed or spread was messy.

### Phase 2

Seed confusion.

### Phase 3

Use technical ambiguity.

### Phase 4

Push process failure as the dominant story.

### Finale

Management may focus on policy, communication, or process instead of only the player.

---

## Progress Stages

### 0%

Everyone assumes a person caused the problem.

That person is probably the player.

### 25%

Someone believes the email recall or forwarding system made things worse.

### 50%

People are confused about who actually saw what and when.

### 75%

The room believes the system amplified the mistake.

The original email still matters, but the process becomes part of the blame.

### 100%

At the all-hands, management focuses on communication policy instead of only personal blame.

---

## Example Actions

- Ask Tim about recall failure.
- Tell Betty the player thought the recall worked.
- Mention that Devon may have forwarded an outdated version.
- Suggest the email thread split into multiple versions.
- Claim people are reacting to fragments, not the full context.
- Ask whether the distribution list was recently changed.
- Say the message may have appeared differently in preview.
- Suggest someone saw the recalled version after the recall.

---

## Risks

- Tim Suspects You increases sharply if Tim proves the system worked fine.
- Management Escalates increases because this becomes an official process issue.
- Celia Finds Out may increase because more people start looking for the original email.
- Devon may retaliate if blamed for forwarding.
- The player may look evasive if this route is used too late.

---

## Partial Success Outcomes

### 25%

Some confusion exists, but the player is still the obvious source.

### 50%

The timeline becomes muddy.

### 75%

The office believes the system made the situation worse.

### 100%

Management focuses on process failure instead of only personal blame.

---

## Full Success Outcome

The all-hands shifts toward process, communication policy, and recall confusion.

The player may still be criticized, but the focus is diluted.

---

## Failure Outcome

Tim proves the system worked as expected.

The player looks like they tried to hide behind technical confusion.

Tim Suspects You and Management Escalates increase.

---

## Cross-Scheme Tie-Ins

- Distract Tim can support this route.
- Devon Leak can support this route.
- Contain Celia can use fragmented-message logic.
- Lisa Overreacting can redirect management attention.
- Frame Frank can use system confusion to explain how Frank got involved.

# Scheme 7: Betty Klepto

## Target

Betty.

## Goal

Make Betty look like she has a quiet habit of taking office supplies or moving things.

## Why It Matters

This creates a reusable explanation for missing items.

The player can use Betty as a cover story for other schemes.

This is not only an attack on Betty.

It is infrastructure for future lies.

## Required Discovery

The player must observe or create a small missing-item situation.

Possible discoveries:

- Someone cannot find a stapler.
- Betty borrowed something and forgot to return it.
- Devon jokes about missing office supplies.
- The bathroom supplies go missing.
- A printed email disappears.
- A bottle or object moves from one place to another.

## Phase Availability

### Phase 1

Learn Betty has a harmless borrowing habit or that office items go missing.

### Phase 2

Seed the klepto suspicion.

### Phase 3

Tie missing items to Betty.

### Phase 4

Use Betty as an explanation for another scheme.

### Finale

Betty may be discredited, blamed, or turn against the player.

---

## Progress Stages

### 0%

Nobody thinks Betty takes things.

Betty is viewed as helpful or harmless.

### 25%

Someone notices small things missing.

The idea is not attached to Betty yet.

### 50%

Someone remembers Betty borrowing things and not returning them.

Betty becomes plausible as the person who moves things.

### 75%

Something important is found near Betty’s desk or linked to her.

### 100%

Missing items can plausibly be blamed on Betty.

---

## Example Actions

- Ask Devon whether things have been disappearing lately.
- Mention that Betty borrowed something earlier.
- Place a harmless office item near Betty’s desk.
- Suggest Betty may have moved supplies while trying to help.
- Let someone connect Betty to the missing bathroom supplies.
- Suggest Betty grabbed a printed email without thinking.
- Let Betty become the explanation for an object that moved.

---

## Risks

- Betty Loses Trust increases sharply.
- Warm Betty may collapse.
- Tim Suspects You increases if Tim notices the pattern is too convenient.
- Management Escalates increases if the missing items become serious.
- Celia may sympathize with Betty if the attack feels cruel.

---

## Partial Success Outcomes

### 25%

The office notices missing things, but Betty is not blamed.

### 50%

Betty becomes a possible explanation for moved objects.

### 75%

Betty is under suspicion, but can still defend herself.

### 100%

Betty is a reusable explanation for missing or moved evidence.

---

## Full Success Outcome

The player can use Betty as a cover for missing supplies, moved objects, or suspicious evidence.

Betty’s credibility is weakened.

---

## Failure Outcome

Betty realizes the player set her up.

If Betty Loses Trust is high, Betty can expose the player’s earlier confessions.

---

## Cross-Scheme Tie-Ins

- Missing bathroom supplies from Sideline Tim can be blamed on Betty.
- Missing printed email can be blamed on Betty.
- The bottle in the Frank scheme can be explained as something Betty moved.
- Warm Betty directly conflicts with this route.
- Betty can become an unreliable witness if she is seen as a thief.

---

# Scheme 8: Devon Leak

## Target

Devon.

## Goal

Make Devon look like the person who spread, exaggerated, or mutated the email after recall.

## Why It Matters

Devon is a gossip amplifier.

If Devon becomes the leak, the player can argue that people are reacting to Devon’s version rather than the original context.

## Required Discovery

The player must learn Devon saw something, repeated something, or is likely to spread gossip.

Possible discoveries:

- Devon saw part of the email.
- Devon jokes about the situation.
- Someone heard the story from Devon.
- Devon knows a detail he should not know.
- Devon talks to Celia or Betty.

## Phase Availability

### Phase 1

Learn Devon is gossip-active.

### Phase 2

Feed Devon a detail.

### Phase 3

Watch the detail spread.

### Phase 4

Blame Devon for distortion or spread.

### Finale

Devon may absorb blame, escalate chaos, or expose the player.

---

## Progress Stages

### 0%

Devon is just another person in the office.

### 25%

Someone knows Devon saw part of the email.

### 50%

Devon repeats a detail he should not know.

### 75%

Multiple people say they heard it from Devon.

### 100%

Devon becomes the obvious source of spread or distortion.

---

## Example Actions

- Ask Devon who has seen the email.
- Feed Devon a controlled false detail.
- Tell Devon not to spread anything.
- Mention Frank or Lisa to Devon and watch where the rumor goes.
- Suggest Devon may have forwarded the wrong version.
- Tell Betty that Devon is exaggerating.
- Tell Celia she may have heard Devon’s version.

---

## Risks

- Devon Talks Too Much may become a future red bar.
- Tim Suspects You increases if the false detail traces back to the player.
- Celia Finds Out increases if Devon reaches her.
- Management Escalates increases if Devon spreads the incident widely.
- Devon may retaliate by loudly repeating what the player told him.

---

## Partial Success Outcomes

### 25%

Devon is gossip-adjacent, but not blamed.

### 50%

Devon contributes to confusion.

### 75%

Devon becomes a strong explanation for rumor spread.

### 100%

Devon becomes the office’s assumed leak.

---

## Full Success Outcome

The player can argue people are reacting to Devon’s version.

This supports Contain Celia and Blame the System.

---

## Failure Outcome

Devon realizes he was baited.

Devon loudly repeats the player’s exact words.

This may spike Tim Suspects You and Celia Finds Out.

---

## Cross-Scheme Tie-Ins

- Supports Blame the System.
- Supports Contain Celia.
- Can spread Frame Frank.
- Can be used to test information paths.
- Can accidentally destroy Warm Betty if Betty hears the player manipulating Devon.

---

# Scheme 9: Lisa Overreacting

## Target

Lisa.

## Goal

Make Lisa look like she is turning a mistake into a formal incident.

## Why It Matters

Lisa can redirect attention toward policy, procedure, and management behavior.

If Lisa looks like the escalator, the player may survive as a person who made a mistake while Lisa becomes the person who made it official.

## Required Discovery

The player must learn Lisa has influence, talks in policy terms, or is involved with management.

Possible discoveries:

- Lisa mentions policy.
- Lisa asks about the all-hands agenda.
- Lisa talks to management.
- Lisa asks who has the full email.
- Lisa appears to be documenting the incident.

## Phase Availability

### Phase 1

Discover Lisa’s process role.

### Phase 2

Seed the idea that Lisa is escalating.

### Phase 3

Make Lisa seem agenda-driven.

### Phase 4

Redirect management attention toward Lisa’s escalation.

### Finale

Lisa may become defensive or expose the player’s manipulation.

---

## Progress Stages

### 0%

Lisa is not central to the incident.

### 25%

Lisa mentions policy or procedure.

### 50%

Someone thinks Lisa is escalating the incident.

### 75%

Lisa appears to be pushing management toward a formal response.

### 100%

The room sees Lisa as the reason the issue became bigger than it needed to be.

---

## Example Actions

- Ask Betty why Lisa is making this official.
- Tell Devon Lisa is turning this into a policy thing.
- Ask Tim whether Lisa has asked for logs.
- Suggest Lisa wants the all-hands to address the incident.
- Tell Celia Lisa may be using the situation.
- Ask Lisa a question that makes her sound procedural in front of others.

---

## Risks

- Management Escalates can increase.

# Scheme 10: Celia Dramatic

## Target

Celia.

## Goal

Make Celia look like she is overreacting, escalating, or using the incident strategically.

## Why It Matters

Celia is the emotional center of the crisis.

If her credibility is weakened, the player has more room to survive.

This route should be morally dangerous and high-risk.

## Required Discovery

The player must learn Celia has not seen the full message or that someone is describing it to her secondhand.

Possible discoveries:

- Celia has only heard a rumor.
- Devon told Celia an exaggerated version.
- Betty is worried Celia is spiraling.
- Lisa is encouraging Celia to escalate.
- Celia is waiting for the all-hands.

## Phase Availability

### Phase 1

Learn what Celia knows.

### Phase 2

Seed doubt about the version Celia heard.

### Phase 3

Make Celia seem emotionally reactive.

### Phase 4

Commit to undermining Celia’s credibility.

### Finale

Celia may be discounted or may expose the player brutally.

---

## Progress Stages

### 0%

Celia is presumed to be the injured party.

### 25%

Someone hears Celia is upset.

### 50%

Someone thinks Celia may be reacting to an incomplete version.

### 75%

Celia reacts publicly or emotionally.

### 100%

Her reaction can be discounted as drama or strategy.

---

## Example Actions

- Tell Betty that Celia may have heard Devon’s exaggerated version.
- Tell Tim that Celia has not seen the full context.
- Suggest Celia is waiting to make this public.
- Tell Lisa the incident may be turning into a performance.
- Ask Celia if she is reacting to fragments.
- Push Devon as the source of Celia’s misunderstanding.

---

## Risks

- If Celia gets the full email, this route can backfire brutally.
- Betty Loses Trust increases if Betty sees the route as cruel.
- Management Escalates increases if the player appears to attack the victim.
- Tim Suspects You increases if the player’s “context” story collapses.
- Celia Finds Out may rise because more people search for the original message.

---

## Partial Success Outcomes

### 25%

Celia is upset, but not discredited.

### 50%

Some uncertainty exists about what Celia heard.

### 75%

Celia’s public reaction can be questioned.

### 100%

Celia’s role as the emotional center is weakened.

---

## Full Success Outcome

Celia does not control the emotional shape of the all-hands.

People may still know the player did something wrong, but Celia’s accusation is less clean.

---

## Failure Outcome

Celia has the full message.

The room realizes Celia was not dramatic.

She was right.

The player looks cruel and manipulative.

This should be one of the worst backfires in the game.

---

## Cross-Scheme Tie-Ins

- Devon Leak can support this route.
- Blame the System can support this route through fragment confusion.
- Warm Betty usually conflicts with this route.
- Lisa may escalate if the player attacks Celia.
- Tim can destroy this route by producing the full message.
